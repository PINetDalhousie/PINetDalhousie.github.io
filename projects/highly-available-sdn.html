Highly-available SDNs

Failures are common in computer networks (either data centers, ISPs or cloud provider WANs). If this was not enough, whenever a failure happens, performance requirements of business critical applications are usually violated. These failures are diverse in nature, and range from equipment failures (e.g., a link failure or routing issue) to misconfigurations and other human-related problems (e.g., accidents, bugs, bad capacity planning or maintenance failures). In this project, we investigate alternatives to detect, explain and recover from failures as quickly as possible in large scale computer networks. In particular, we focus on recent programmable network architectures (e.g., SDN/OpenFlow and P4).
