SDN/NFV-based Low-power IoT Networks

Low-power IoTs are good candidates for environmental monitoring (e.g., forest fire, humidity at home, or ocean dynamics). Usually, these low-power IoT devices come from different vendors with limited computing, memory, and power capacity and operate over lossy wireless networks; thus, SDN can offer hardware abstraction and network programmability to dynamically cope with the network changes. Also, NFV can enable deploying on-demand virtual functions (e.g., data aggregation) in chosen IoT devices. In this project, we focus on designing novel multi-resource-aware protocols on SDN/NFV platforms. Also, we devise portable architecture benchmarking for the low-power IoT domain. Another focus is on data synchronization and trust in this hostile low-power and lossy networking environment.
