Overview


We are passionate about all aspects of networked systems and applications, but you can find a summary of our main research interests in the above figure. Our projects usually include one or more of those topics. From an application perspective, our projects target smart cities, homes, agriculture, transportation, etc. These applications heavily rely on computer networks and networked systems that we design in collaboration with domain-specific experts. Furthermore, we focus on the needs of Atlantic Canada and thus also prioritize applications like ocean science and energy systems. Our interests are around performance, reliability, and security to realize the above applications in the networking services, where monitoring is orthogonal to these services. Our lab is specialized over time in three broad disciplines: programmable networking (e.g., SDN or NFV), machine learning, and optimization. These technologies have evolved for years, and we are still amazed at how powerful they are. We are big fans of the "networking for x" and "x for networking" jargon, so not rarely you will see an overlap among the technologies we mentioned. Finally, we are eclectic regarding the types of networks we target, ranging from IoT to data centers. That may sound a bit broad to you, but fortunately, the group can count on exceptional experts in both wired and wireless domains.
